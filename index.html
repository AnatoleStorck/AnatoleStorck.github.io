The MEGATRON simulation

My PhD is currently focused on tracking and characterising population III stars in the MEGATRON simulation. This simulation attempts to recreate the early evolution of a Milky Way-like galaxy using supercomputers and advanced physical models.

Splicing dark matter halos

This work looks at how cosmic filaments impact the evolution of dark matter halos. We use "splicing" to insert dark matter halos into different cosmological environments. Doing so allows us to comparatively extract the impact that non-linear structure formation in the environment has on final halo properties.

Binary neutron stars and their gravitational waves

This work focused on characterizing the distributions of double neutron star properties (e.g. frequency, eccentricity) which are observable to the upcoming gravitational wave detector LISA, under different assumptions of the Galactic model.
